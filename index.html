A Place to Share My Latest Research and Findings.

I am a PhD student in the Department of Mining and Materials Engineering at McGill University, with a strong interest in the physics of phase change phenomena. My research integrates physical-based modeling, sensitivity analysis and experiments to advance our fundamental understanding of phase change, particularly the freezing process of pure substances, binary mixtures and porous media. Developing such fundamental frameworks benefits real-world applications in artificial ground freezing, droplet solidification, spray freezing, phase change materials, and cold energy storage to fight against permafrost thawing due to climate change. Since July 2022, I am also a part-time research employee in CanmetENERGY under the Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) working on CO2 ground source heat pump systems for renewable heating and cooling.

I am a Vanier Scholar and a recipient of Fonds de recherche - Nature et technologies (FRQ-NT) Doctoral and Master's scholarships. I obtained my Bachelor of Engineering (First Class Honours) in bioresource engineering from McGill University, where I tutored freshman calculus and physics as a peer mentor, tutorial/laboratory coordinator and teaching assistant for three years. Before my bachelor's study, I grew up in Dalian (a coastal city in Northeast China) and graduated from a Canadian international high school (MLES). For more details, please see my curriculum vitae.

Research Projects

Stefan Problem

Stefan problem tracks the moving solid-liquid interface to model freezing and melting. We develop approximate analytical solutions to Stefan problems by singular perturbation method and asymptotic analysis. Our analytical solutions can be several orders of magnitude faster than conventional numerical methods.

Latest publications: Applied Mathematics and Computation, International Journal of Heat and Mass Transfer

Droplet Solidification and Spray Freezing

Understanding the freezing process of droplets and sprays can be useful in storing food and pharmaceutical products (e.g., vaccines) as well as heating in underground mines. We combine mathematical modeling, sensitivity analysis and optimization to accurately predict droplet solidification and spray freezing at both microscopic and macroscopic scales.

Latest publications: International Journal of Heat and Mass Transfer, Crystal Growth & Design

Artificial Ground Freezing

Artificial ground freezing (AGF) is a geotechnical support method that helps the stabilization and hydraulic sealing of the ground. We establish reduced-order models to capture multiphysics phenomena in AGF, which can further be used as a mitigation strategy to fight against permafrost thawing due to climate change.

Latest publications: Applied Thermal Engineering, Journal of Heat Transfer, Tunnelling and Underground Space Technology (Review Paper)

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy is a renewable energy source with great potential in lowering environmental impact and greenhouse gases emissions. We model geothermal borehole heat exchangers (BHEs) via analytical methods to optimize the thermal design and performances.

Latest publications: Applied Thermal Engineering, Applied Energy, Applied Sciences (Special Issue, Open Access)
